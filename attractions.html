From ancient palaces to mythical caves and charming villages, Crete’s attractions are a perfect blend of culture, nature and legend. Discover historical landmarks, scenic wonders and hidden gems that bring the island’s vibrant past and present to life, each with a story worth exploring.

Ancient ruins, scenic spots and charming villages. Crete’s attractions mix history and beauty. Explore caves, monuments and natural wonders that tell the island’s story in the most unforgettable way.

Just a short boat ride from Plaka, Spinalonga is a small island with sea views, haunting ruins and powerful stories from its days as a leper colony.

In the heart of Agios Nikolaos, Voulismeni Lake is said to be Athena’s bathing spot. Surrounded by cafés and cliffs. It’s a magical place for a scenic stroll or peaceful pause.

View on Maps

Tucked into the hills above Agios Nikolaos, Kritsa is a charming village known for its cobbled streets, handmade textiles and peaceful traditional vibe.

View on Maps

Dikteon Cave is a mythical site on the Lasithi Plateau, believed to be Zeus’s birthplace with stunning rock formations and a magical atmosphere.

Dive into Mediterranean marine life at Cretaquarium in Heraklion. From jellyfish to sharks it’s an exciting family-friendly escape beneath the sea.

Step into the heart of Minoan civilization at Knossos. Just outside Heraklion, this ancient palace holds vibrant frescoes, royal halls and the legendary Labyrinth of the Minotaur.

Between mountains and sea, Toplou Monastery is a 15th-century gem with rich history and sacred icons. Explore its peaceful courtyards and taste local wine and olive oil.
